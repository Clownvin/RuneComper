Strategies[edit | edit source]

As this achievement can be tedious even if done optimally, it is recommended to do some preparations beforehand to minimise the time and effort expended. Before doing this achievement, it is strongly recommended to finish the Hard Desert achievements for a 10% discount in the reward shop. Alternatively, the player can first get the points needed, then return later to unlock the spell after finishing the Hard Desert achievements.

Requirements[edit | edit source]

  • Level 33 Magic (for Telekinetic Grab) is required to be able to participate in all 4 activities.
  • Be on the standard spellbook.
  • Having a progress hat is required to participate. It can be obtained from the Entrance Guardian and can be freely destroyed and reclaimed.
    • The hat can be equipped to save inventory space.

Getting there[edit | edit source]

  • The fastest way to reach the Mage Training Arena is to use the wicked hood to teleport to the fire altar. Alternatively, use a ring of duelling or passage of the abyss to teleport to Het's Oasis. After teleporting, run northeast to reach the Mage Training Arena.
  • The nearest bank is a bank chest north of Het's Oasis.

General tips[edit | edit source]

  • Level 87 Magic is recommended to be able to cast Lvl-6 Enchant for the Enchanting Chamber. Boosts can be used.
  • Depending on the room, it is recommended to put the relevant spell on an action bar and keybind it. One room, the Enchanting Chamber, has two spells that should be keybound.
  • Since all four elemental runes will be needed, is recommended to bring an elemental battlestaff (can be purchased on the Grand Exchange). Alternatives for ironmen include the mindspike or tower mindspike—which can be converted into a staff that provides any one of the elemental runes—and a mud battlestaff.
  • The player should bring cosmic, nature, and law runes, as they will be needed for various rooms. Optionally add these runes to a large or grasping rune pouch to save inventory space.
  • The Surge ability is useful for several rooms. Wearing a piece of augmented armour with the mobile perk is highly recommended. Having Double Surge and/or Dive unlocked can also be useful.

Telekinetic Theatre[edit | edit source]

For solutions to the maze puzzles, see Mage Training Arena/Puzzle Solutions
  • This will be the longest room of the four due to how long it takes to complete each puzzle. All puzzles require between 7 and 10 casts of Telekinetic Grab. Even with perfect gameplay, the animations of the Telekinetic Grabs and the statue moving are unskippable, resulting in much time spent waiting for the statue to move.
  • The player must stand directly adjacent to one of the walls of the maze for Telekinetic Grab to work. Standing in a corner, or standing one or more squares away from the maze, will cause Telekinetic Grab to not work.
  • The player can surge around the room to save time.
  • Standing one square away from the corner can save time, as the player can just walk two squares to change to the near side, or surge to get to the far side.

Alchemists' Playground[edit | edit source]

The Alchemy interface
  • Using Low Level Alchemy is strongly recommended because it casts faster than High Level Alchemy but still gives the same coins.
  • Keep an eye on the Alchemy Guardian; he will alert the player when the coin values change.
  • For maximum efficiency, always alch the item worth 30 coins; ignore the "free" item unless it is also the 30-coin one.
  • It is possible to alch around 15–20 items before the coin values change and the cabinets reshuffle.
  • There are always 3 empty cabinets adjacent to one another, and 5 with items. The items are always next to each other in the same order as the interface. (It may be helpful to think of the room as a wheel with 8 quadrants. The items are in counterclockwise order.)
  • One way to make this room easier is to orient the camera so that the cabinet is between the player character and the camera. (Use freedom camera in settings and use the mousewheel to move the camera.)
    • Example: if the player finds the adamant helm, the emerald will be directly to the left, and the kiteshield will be directly to the right. If the player reaches the end of the row, keep going around the "wheel".
  • Using Surge can help get around the room faster.
  • If you lobby while you have coins in your inventory they will disappear. Make sure you deposit them before you manually lobby.

Enchanting Chamber[edit | edit source]

World-hopping settings
  • This is the fastest and easiest room. The optimal strategy involves collecting the six dragonstones in the room, enchanting them, Depositing the orbs after enchanting is optional and should be skipped. Hop worlds and repeat.
    • Note: use the settings in the image to hop worlds quickly.
  • Instead of manually picking up the dragonstones, using Telekinetic Grab can save time.
  • It is highly recommended to use Lvl-6 Enchant as it gives the most points per dragonstone.
  • The dragonstones take 5 minutes to respawn, so the player should avoid hopping back to worlds they've already been on.

Creature Graveyard[edit | edit source]

  • This room will deal 200 typeless damage to the player every once in a while. Having an Enhanced Excalibur and some stackable food, like magnetic minnows, can be useful. The player can also wear expensive spices to make the food heal more. Alternatively, using the Rapid Renewal prayer and a regen bracelet allows the player to out-heal the damage. (For more useful items and survival strategies, see here.)
  • Use the west side of the room (there is a bone pile very close to the deposit chute), and use Bones to Bananas to convert the bones.
  • It is important to understand how this room works: there are four types of animals' bones, and each bone is worth a different number of fruit. The player will pick up four bones worth 1 piece of fruit, then four worth 2, then four worth 3, then four worth 4. The bones obtained will always follow this specific repeating cycle.
  • Every 16 fruits deposited into the chute will give 1 pizazz point; any excess fruits deposited will count towards the next point.
  • The player can bring at least one each of blood, death, earth, and water runes in the inventory as placeholders, as this room frequently rewards players with these runes.
  • One strategy to do this room without over-clicking is to bring placeholders and have exactly 20 empty inventory spaces when entering. The player can proceed to always get 8 bones at a time: 4 bones worth 2 fruits and 4 bones worth 3 fruits will yield exactly 20 bananas, and 4 bones worth 4 fruits and 4 bones worth 1 fruit will also yield exactly 20 bananas. Thus, the player should adjust their cycle to start on either the first bone worth two fruits or the first bone worth four fruits. Then, they can rely on this process: click 8 times, cast Bones to Bananas to always get 20 bananas, deposit them, and repeat the process.
