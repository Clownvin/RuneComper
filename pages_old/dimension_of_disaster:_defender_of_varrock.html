The majority of this quest involves getting a code. To save significant time on replays, write it down when you get it.

Starting out[edit | edit source]

Arrav and Ellamaria in New Varrock Palace's garden.

Begin the quest by speaking to Reldo in New Varrock Castle's library, the room west of Arrav. He will tell you that he is the only remaining human in New Varrock; Zemouregal kept him alive to research the Shield of Arrav. He has told Zemouregal most of what he learned, but kept undisclosed a vital piece of information: only descendants of Varrock's first king can unlock the full power of the shield.

Reldo has not learned who can wield the shield's power yet. Suggest to him that it is Arrav, and Reldo will tell you that this might be possible, but Zemouregal has Arrav under his control. Head east to the garden and speak to Arrav. He will say that he is unable to talk, but ask him if he would like to bring down Zemouregal. Arrav will say he is foretold to be under control forever, but will nonetheless be interested in your offer.

Gathering information[edit | edit source]

Return to Reldo and relay the news: Arrav is interested, but unable. Reldo notes that despite mind control requiring 100% attention, Zemouregal is able to do other activities; there must be another source, an artefact, which can be destroyed.

Search the library for clues. You will find Matters of the Heart on a book shelf in the far north-west corner of the room. (Note: if your brightness settings are too low, you may not be able to see the row of bookshelves that line the entire west wall of the library). Return to Reldo and show him the book. Zemouregal is using an obsidian artefact to strengthen his power over Arrav. Reldo tells you that Zemouregal keeps all his valuables in his treasure room, which is upstairs on the 1st floor[UK]2nd floor[US]. (If your character says they do not have a reason to go up the stairs yet, then it means you did not show the book to Reldo.) You will need to make your way upstairs; however only zombies are able to enter the upper premises of the palace.

Becoming your enemy[edit | edit source]

Listening in on Zemouregal.

Reldo suggests speaking to Thessalia to fashion up a disguise. Thessalia stocks a full set of zombie clothes in her shop. The full set costs 500 Zemomarks. If you are replaying the quest and have purchased the cosmetic override version, then it can be used instead.

Return to New Varrock Palace. While you are still downstairs, go to the kitchen in the east wing and pick up two vials (only one is needed for subsequent play-throughs unless you wish to fix the tin soldier again). Wearing your zombie disguise, head upstairs via the south-east staircase of the castle. Kill armoured zombies and collect the red mist they produce with the vial, creating a vial of red mist. You will need two.

Head north to the balcony and listen in on Zemouregal to learn that the code to the door is protected by a human detection spell, and that only the tin man controlled with the scrying pool can pass through.

Inspect the decapitated tin soldier, located southwest from where you eavesdrop on the conversation, by going through the red barrier, going around the courtyard, through the second red barrier to the west and into the southern room to investigate the tin soldier who is located on the operating table.

Booting up[edit | edit source]

Repairing the tin soldier.

If you have written down the code from previous attempts, you can skip the tin soldier building part for replay attempts

  • The head can be obtained by speaking to the trial announcer outside the city's eastern bank.
  • Tin ore (3) can either be obtained from zombie citizens or purchased from Sani in the western part of the city. Tin ore can also be looted from certain chests if you have not done so already.
  • The runes required (4 fire runes and 1 nature rune) can also be obtained from zombies around the city, or purchased from Aubury in the south eastern part of the city. These runes can also be looted from certain chests if you have not done so already. A staff of fire can be used instead of the fire runes.

Once all the materials have been acquired, return to the tin soldier upstairs inside the castle. You will need to repair the tin soldier with a tin soldier head and fix the damage by using three pieces of tin ore, which will be automatically superheated directly onto the torso. Do not use the superheat spell on the tin ore directly. Finally, use a red mist on him by clicking on the tin man and you will be prompted whether you want to use the red mist on him.

Bypass human detection[edit | edit source]

Using the Tin soldier to go past the human detection spell.

Leave the tin soldier room. Return to the room in which you killed an armoured zombie and kill another one to collect the red mists for entering red barriers. Head north along the corridor, past the red doorway you went through previously, and turn west and walk along the hallway. Enter the northwestern room which contains a Zilyana statue via the red barrier. Investigate the scrying pool in the northeast corner and select the option which will let you operate the tin man you just repaired. The screen will turn purple, the tin man will be standing in front of the table to which he used to be strapped, and you will be able to control him like you typically do with your own character.

Once in control, navigate out of the room where the tin soldier started, turn north, and go west through the doorway at the end of the hall, passing effortlessly through the 'Human Detection Spell' into the room. Search Zemouregal's lab desk to obtain the key code written on a small card between the pages of the book on the desk. This code is different for all players; however, it will remain the same for subsequent playthroughs of the quest. If you forget the code you can investigate the scrying pool (alternatively the code is updated in your quest journal). To stop using the tin soldier, return to his original room and lie on the table or simply select the option available to disconnected from the tin soldier. While at the scrying pool, it can be used six more times to complete the 'Scrying Game' achievement and earn 20 zemomarks in the process.

Unlock the safe[edit | edit source]

An example of the puzzle. The example code is 'EIHC', thus the final decoder strip must be placed on the C, revealing that the number is 2.

With the code, head to the southeastern room north of the staircase hallway, which contains training dummies and a stage, and pick up a set of decoder strips from the crate in the southeast corner. Return to the scrying pool room and head to the vault in the north west corner. The vault door uses a decoder puzzle as its lock. To obtain the number code, place the corresponding decoder strips on the right on to the letters on the left as they appeared in order. For example, if the code is 'BAGD', drag strip 1 onto B, strip 2 onto A, strip 3 onto G, and strip 4 onto D. The strips will cover up all but one number. The numbers shown by the strips, in the order of the strips, will be your code. In the above example, if the numbers left uncovered were 3 on B (#1), 9 on A (#2), 5 on G (#3), and 3 on D (#4), then the code to the vault would be 3953.

Use the dial on the vault to enter the code. Use the arrows on the left column up and down to increase/decrease the current number, and right arrow to enter the number in. Once all 4 numbers are entered, the vault will open.

Freedom[edit | edit source]

Enter the vault and smash the black prism.

Exit the vault by selecting 'Enter' on the doorway, return downstairs to the centre courtyard and speak to Arrav. He will say he is feeling different; he is no longer under Zemouregal's mind control.

Congratulations! Quest complete.

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • While observing the garden of hostility in the scrying pool, Arrav will sing a song from The Wizard of Oz about the tin man and having a heart.
  • Searching the bookcases will occasionally yield the message 'a walkthrough for Dimension of Disaster', which is a reference to players using quest guides to complete quests.
  • While eavesdropping on Zemouregal on the 1st floor[UK]2nd floor[US], Sharathteerk constantly lampshades how it's a bad idea to reveal such important details.
  • When asking Reldo about King Roald and Aeonisig Raispher during the initial conversation, he will say that when Aeonisig tried to wield it, "Nothing interesting happened." This is a reference to the message received when using items in an incompatible manner.
  • Ellamaria says that "One day, I’ll have a man-eating garden of barbed creepers, bloater spores and gobbler audreys." A man-eating plant named Audrey features in The Little Shop of Horrors.
  • After accepting the quest, Reldo states he admires the player's "chutzpah", which is the Yiddish word for audacity or extreme self-confidence.
