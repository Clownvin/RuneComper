Unwelcome Guests is the third quest in the Fort Forinthry quest series.

Although the quest is listed under "None" when sorting by "Combat", it requires dealing with a group of fetid zombies of combat level 66.

Walkthrough[edit | edit source]

Starting off[edit | edit source]

Talk to Overseer Siv in the Command Centre. You learn that she wants to increase security in the fort because of events in the previous quest. Siv will complain about the Raptor who has wandered in unchallenged. You agree to deal with him.

Head over to the western side of the main gate and speak to the Raptor. You learn that the undead have been seen in the north and are capable of destroying the fort. You agree to help.

Prepare yourself for combat, then head through the northern gate that leads to the wilderness to continue.

Beyond the northern gate[edit | edit source]

Slay ten fetid zombies to the west whilst the Raptor deals with the skeletons to the north. They should not be too hard to kill, and they only use melee attacks.

Once finished, a cutscene will occur and you will end up back in the fort.

Fortifying the walls[edit | edit source]

Talk to the Raptor again inside the fort. The Raptor will ask you to fortify the fort as they will return because the undead can easily pass your wall. Speak to Bill to learn how.

Bill will provide building supplies for the defences around the perimeter. Leave via the northern gate and build the defences situated around the walls. There are six to build. Be mindful that the fetid zombies surrounding the fort will attack you whilst you build, therefore bring protection such as armour or prayer. Unlike the other fort hotspots these are built much faster, requiring 3 sets of progress (each one filling ~33.3% of the bar) and do not need any materials. Having Auto-retaliate off is helpful to avoid getting distracted by the attacks.

Building the Guardhouse[edit | edit source]

Guardhouse tier 1

Speak to the Raptor, followed by Overseer Siv who will inform you of guards she has recruited - dark warriors who fled their fortress to avoid getting embroiled in a Zamorakian conflict. These guards now need a station. Speak to Bill who will then draw up plans for a Guardhouse, which you will build. This requires 14 maple frames and six stone wall segments. You only need to build tier 1 to progress.

Once finished, speak to the Raptor or Overseer Siv and a cutscene will occur. The Raptor will then want to find the source of the undead.

Source of the undead[edit | edit source]

Zemouregal summons some Armoured Phantoms.

Go through the northern gate again and another cutscene will occur. More undead have arrived and you will need to kill ten fetid zombies to the west.

Once completed, an Armoured Phantom appears and attacks the Raptor. The Raptor will defeat it and point out it came from the north.

You and the Raptor will walk through the Wilderness Crypt Entrance. Inside, you will find Zemouregal is up to no good again, though by his claim he already has what he came for. He summons a large amount of Armoured Phantoms before leaving, but both of you manage to escape.

Finishing up[edit | edit source]

Back at the fort, speak to the Raptor, who will remain and help protect the fort from further threats while keeping the new guards in line with his presence.

Quest complete!

Rewards[edit | edit source]

  • 1 quest point
  • Ability to build all three tiers of the Fort Forinthry Guardhouse and Grove.
  • The Raptor will operate as a Slayer master inside Fort Forinthry.
  • Access to the crypt north of Fort Forinthry.
  • 2 Treasure Hunter keys (Ironman accounts will not receive these)

Achievements[edit | edit source]

  • Unwelcome Guests ( 0) – Complete this quest.

Required for completing[edit | edit source]

Unwelcome Guests is not currently required for any quests or miniquests.

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • Upon completion of the quest, the Adventurer's Log states: I found the source of undead attacking Fort Forinthry and helped the raptor setup a defensive front.
  • In the post-quest dialogue with Fort Guard, the guard will say "Better dead than red" to show that he would rather die than be Zamorakian. This line is a reference to the Cold War Era anti-communist slogan.
  • The Quest Overview incorrectly states that teak frames are needed instead of maple frames.
