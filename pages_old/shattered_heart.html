Shattered Heart is a Distraction and Diversion centred on collecting strange and golden rocks. It was first mentioned on 12 February 2010 in the developer blog "A History of Non-violence." Golden rocks were added with the addition of Prifddinas on 22 September 2014.

Introduction & Requirements[edit | edit source]

Shattered Heart is focused upon obtaining two strange rocks for each of the non-combat related skills and two golden rocks for Prifddinas-related skills. Since a player may end up having up to 30 strange rocks and 32 golden rocks for their corresponding statues, it is advised to seek out Barnabus Hurma, who will give the player a statue collection bag to store the strange and golden rocks in, thus freeing up space in the bank or inventory. Even though both rocks in a pair are identical, they do not stack in the player's bank.

The rocks are acquired simply by training their respective skills and will randomly appear in the player's inventory. If the statue collection bag is in the inventory or equipped, the rocks will be sent there instead. Upon receiving a rock of any type, the chance of receiving another identical rock will be reduced while the chance of receiving rocks for other skills remains unchanged. The reduced drop chance will always apply only to the skill from which the player's last rock was obtained. Thus, if players are trying to find the most rocks in the shortest amount of time, whenever they receive a rock they should immediately switch to training a different skill for a different type of rock.

If a player's inventory is full and they receive a rock, it will be sent to the player's bank. If the player's bank is also full, rocks will attempt to replace the item that the player was acquiring with the respective activity. If this occurs, the following message appears in chat:

  • " Something odd happens to the (item name) and you are left with a strange rock."
  • " Something odd happens to the (item name) and you are left with a golden rock."

Carrying or wielding the statue collection bag will send the rocks straight into the bag, giving the message:

  • " You find a strange rock and add it to your bag." or " You find a golden rock and add it to your bag."

Only members are able to find rocks of either type. There are no minimum level requirements for the discovery of strange rocks. Golden rocks, however, indirectly require the player to have at least level 75 in all Prifddinas-related skills as completion of the quest Plague's End is required to access Prifddinas. Players may complete the set of strange and golden rocks as many times as they wish; however, players must first turn in the complete set of all strange or golden rocks before they can begin receiving a new set of the respective rocks.

Starting and Stopping[edit | edit source]

The D&D can be said to be completed when 900 strange rocks and 1024 golden rocks have been turned in, making it unique among D&Ds in the sense that it can be finished. However, even after the replica statues have been completed, players will continue to receive rocks. To stop receiving rocks of either type, players can opt out at any time by speaking to a Varrock Museum archaeologist (the six workers sitting at desks to the south on the ground floor[UK]1st floor[US]) or Auron Ithell for strange and golden rocks respectively. Players must receive the plinth before they can stop receiving rocks. This is not advised, however, as players would be missing out on free experience for completing additional statues. If players opt to not receive rocks, the earlier mentioned NPCs can be spoken to again to resume receiving the corresponding rocks.

Dahmaroc Statue[edit | edit source]

Main article: Statue of Dahmaroc (Varrock Museum)
The statue of Dahmaroc crumbling.

When a pair of strange rocks of any particular skill have been obtained, they can be used on the plinth in the Varrock Museum to build a statue of Dahmaroc. He was a mage in the Second Age, when runes were quite rare. Runecrafting wasn't yet discovered, so mages had to use what Guthix supplied Gielinor with. Battles were fought everywhere, and some remaining artefacts can be found while skilling in the form of strange rocks. Dahmaroc was a scholar, a skilled crafter, and was cursed and killed while seeking runestones.

When his statue is rebuilt, Dahmaroc comes alive for a short moment. The curse then causes the statue to crumble, awarding a replica statue piece and allowing the player to build a new one. Using pairs of replica statue pieces to build the replica statue in the player's player-owned house awards the Stone's Throw Away achievement, which is a trimmed completionist cape requirement.

Statue of Rhiannon[edit | edit source]

Main article: Statue of Rhiannon

If a pair of golden rocks have been obtained, they can be used on the statue plinth in the Tower of Voices in Prifddinas to build a second statue, the statue of Rhiannon. Similarly to the Dahmaroc statue, experience is gained for each pair of rocks that are added to the plinth. However, the pairs of Melee, Ranged, and Magic rocks allow the player to choose which combat skill they wish to receive experience in. Building the statue of Rhiannon 32 times awards the Who Will Be Her Lover? achievement, which is a trimmed completionist cape requirement.

Additional information, Hints, & Tips[edit | edit source]

Rocks can be a good way of obtaining additional experience in "slow" skills, such as Runecrafting, if the activity is only undertaken for the time required to obtain the two rocks. At level 70, the experience reward from the rocks may be greater than the experience received from the time spent on the activity itself, where it is possible to obtain 5,000 experience from the rocks gained from the time taken to earn 500 experience actually Runecrafting (death runes at 10 experience each without the use of the Abyss). This is because each crafted essence is treated as an individual action, rather than the crafting of all essence counting as a single action. On the other hand, with skills that have a high experience per action, this situation can be reversed. For instance, at skill level 70 Hunter it is quite possible to have to earn 25,000-50,000 experience hunting Carnivorous Chinchompas at 265 experience each in order to get the pair of rocks for 5,000 experience. This is only true if players switch to training a different skill after receiving a rock because, as previously mentioned, the chance of receiving a second rock of the same type as the next rock is greatly reduced.

The higher a player's current level in the relevant skill, the more experience they will receive as a reward. There is a piece of the Dahmaroc statue for nearly all of the non-combat skills. Each one is made up of two strange rocks found while training. Once a pair has been obtained, no additional strange rocks will be received from that skill until the statue has been completed or the rocks are destroyed. It is not possible to add one rock to the statues, the rocks must be added in pairs.

Many activities that are valid training methods, such as stringing bows, do not provide a chance of finding a rock. Similar to how skilling pets are found, the chance of finding a rock is rolled on each action rather than after a set amount of experience is gained. As a result, some "power training" techniques such as farming trees, while delivering a large amount of experience, actually give comparatively few chances at finding a rock. To maximize the chance of finding strange and golden rocks when training a skill, it is recommended to try the method that performs actions the fastest; for example, big net fishing awards strange rocks faster than lobster caging because the catch rate per hour is higher, even though failing to catch a fish may also yield a rock.

While rocks can be obtained from Woodcutting, Fishing, and Mining while gaining approval rating for Managing Miscellania, these activities cannot be continued on Miscellania once 100% approval has been reached. For players who wish to complete the replica statue as quickly as possible, and for those who do not wish to spend time training a particular skill, it may be more prudent to keep a store of cheap low level items such as maple logs, gold bars, raw tuna, clean guam, eye of newt, etc to quickly and efficiently obtain rocks without devoting undue time to the skills in question.

Activity doubling effects awarded from completion of the various exploration achievements, such as double ore smithing with Varrock armour or double pickpocketing with high Agility, give two chances at finding a rock. This appears to be the cause behind reports of players receiving two rocks from a single action.[source needed] The Voice of Seren provides an increased chance of finding golden rocks in the respective clan's skill, but does not apply to strange rocks even if a pair exists for the same skill.

Rewards[edit | edit source]

Summary[edit | edit source]

  • Experience in a skill for each pair of rocks added to the statues. The experience depends on the skill level and from which skill the rocks were received.
  • For Dahmaroc's statue:
    • 10 kudos when the statue is completed for the first time.
    • A statue plinth for a study in the player's player-owned house when completing the statue for the first time.
    • A replica statue piece each time the statue is completed that can be added in pairs to the plinth in the study of the player's player-owned house.
  • For the statue of Rhiannon:
    • A replica statue piece gets added to the Max Guild's plinth each time the statue is completed.

Experience reward formula[edit | edit source]

The formula for the statue of Dahmaroc is Experience = x 2 − 2 x + 100 {\displaystyle {\text{Experience}}=x^{2}-2x+100} , where x is the skill level. An alternate formula, showing the same answer, is Experience = 99 + ( x − 1 ) 2 {\displaystyle {\text{Experience}}=99+(x-1)^{2}} .

The statue of Rhiannon provides a 20% increase from the experience given by Dahmaroc's statue, so the experience given is Experience = 1.2 x 2 − 2.4 x + 120 {\displaystyle {\text{Experience}}=1.2x^{2}-2.4x+120} , where x {\displaystyle x} is the skill level.

Achievements[edit | edit source]

  • Stone's Throw Away ( 50) – Complete the Dahmaroc statue in your player-owned house.
  • Rhian in Gold - The Original ( 50) – Fully build the statue of Rhiannon in the Tower of Voices at least once.
  • Who Will Be Her Lover? ( 75) – Complete the replica statue of Rhiannon in the Max Guild garden.

Update history[edit | edit source]

This information has been compiled as part of the update history project. Some updates may not be included—see here for how to help out!
  • patch 20 February 2023 (Update):
    • Players are no longer guaranteed to receive strange and golden rocks instantly after starting construction in Fort Forinthry.
  • ninja 13 September 2021 (Update):
    • The weekly limit on the Shattered Heart D&D has been removed.
      • Subsequently, the Stones throw away achievement is no longer time gated.
    • Strange and Golden Rocks can now be added to their respective statues directly from the Statue collection bag or the Backpack.
      • Added feedback messaging upon trying to add Rocks without currently possessing any.
      • Strange and Golden Rocks will now be sent to the Bank if you don't have enough Backpack space.
      • If your Bank is full, the Rock will either replace the last item received or be lost entirely, depending on the skilling method.
      • Info-boxes will now be displayed when receiving Strange or Golden Rocks.
    • The Statue Collection Bag can now be filled via the Bank while it's in the Backpack.
  • patch 26 October 2020 (Update):
    • God Statue and Strange Rock Statue interfaces no longer interfere with the size of the chat window when opened.
  • patch 2 November 2010 (Update):
    • Receiving a Shattered Heart statue bag and turning off the receiving of statue parts no longer conflict with each other.
  • patch 15 March 2010 (Update):
    • Added the chance of getting strange rocks from a few places that were missing it.
  • update 3 March 2010 (Update):
    • Added to game.

Trivia[edit | edit source]

Congratulation message sent to early completers of Shattered Heart
  • If the statue is examined during the cutscene, this message will appear: "A completed statue of a cursed Second Age mage."
  • Although very rare, obtaining two rocks of the same skill type for the same statue at once is possible.[source needed]
  • After completing the replica statue, the player's adventurer's log will read: "Each time I rebuilt the statue of Dahmaroc in Varrock Museum, the archaeologists granted me a replica piece. It was a lot of work, but today I completed my own statue at home."
  • When a strange rock is received while having a pet rock in the inventory, a message appears saying: Your pet rock is so happy to have stone-based company that it jiggles about a little in your pack.
  • The experience given as rewards does count toward reducing the experience needed to make another trip to perform Tears of Guthix collection.
  • The advent of Shattered Heart meant that many players started gaining levels in little used skills, making the overall rankings table considerably more competitive.
  • Players who completed the player-owned house statue of Dahmaroc early were sent messages of congratulations from Mod Maz and Mod Emilee.
  • When adding multiple pairs of rocks at once, if a level is gained in a skill, the normal chat messages will not appear until all pairs of rocks have been added from the inventory.
  • The name "Dahmaroc" may be a play on words meaning "Damn a rock", since the mage Dahmaroc was cursed into stone.
  • If the study is removed while the Dahmaroc statue still stands in it, then when a new study is built and a new plinth is obtained and set up, the new statue should be as complete as the previous one. However, if the statue is removed from the study, all replica pieces added so far will be lost.

Glitches and changed features[edit | edit source]

  • A bug on the release date allowed players to get multiple stones while fishing. This has now been fixed.
  • Smelting gold ore did not yield stones on the day of release.
  • In the early days of Shattered Heart, the chance of receiving a Runecrafting rock was counted per use of altar and not per essence. For the highest chance of getting a rock, essence had to be dropped and bound one piece at a time. Mod Maz confirmed that this has been fixed.
  • In the first two weeks the fastest way of getting agility rocks was to repeat an obstacle, notably "climb low wall". This wasn't how Shattered Heart was intended to be played; Jagex changed this so that only completing whole courses has a chance of getting rocks.
  • There was a glitch that made it possible to receive a strange rock on non-members' worlds, resulting in the creation of a members object (although players would still receive the message You find a strange rock.). This glitch has been fixed.
  • On 25 March 2010, players with multiple statues in their house found the statues removed. When finishing their next statue; however, they could retrieve the plinth and all replica pieces they had added before the removal.
  • Prior to an update on 13 September 2021, players could only complete the Dahmaroc statue once per week, therefore it took a minimum of 30 weeks to complete the player-owned house replica statue. This means from the release on the 3 March 2010, the earliest someone could have completed the statue was on the 22 September 2010. Exactly four years later the golden rocks were added.
  • On 19 March 2010 in a recent updates thread, Mod Maz confirmed that the fully assembled statue in the player's player-owned house will be a cosmetic reward only and thus won't serve any extra purposes. After an update on 3 May 2011, the trimmed completionist cape required completing the Dahmaroc statue in a player-owned house, along with some other requirements, making it an extended reward.
