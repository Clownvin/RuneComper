Contract Claws is a mystery and achievement encountered at the Infernal Source Dig Site, wherein the player learns of the thirteen guardian gargoyles of the site, also known as the Malebranche. The player may start this mystery by speaking to Ophiuchus the gargoyle, who may be found in the Vestibule of Futility, the uppermost level of the Source, beside the portal to the Dungeon of Disorder.

Steps[edit | edit source]

Required items[edit | edit source]

In order to acquire the items required for the mystery, players may use this guide. If they already have the item in their bank they may simply withdraw it. The cow(s), red dye, bacon, dragon bones and frog spawn will be consumed by the gargoyles and cannot be returned after the mystery.

Item(s) Steps/Tradeable/methods to acquire
Harlequin cow
or
vanilla, chocolate, and strawberry cow
  • Either breed any type of cow with the "Shiny!" trait, or purchase from another player.
    Any cow that has "Shiny!" in the tooltip will work, even if it is a specific colour i.e. a Shiny! vanilla cow. Bulls also work in this scenario.
  • Alternatively, buy the 3 coloured cows from the Grand Exchange - vanilla, chocolate, and strawberry cow, then "check" them to make them usable for the mystery.
Dog (including the corgi, the Lorehound, the abyssal hound, Blue, or Blazehound)
  1. Go to the Pet Shop in Taverley and ask about the "puppy in the window".
  2. You will be allowed to select a breed of dog and buy it.

Alternatively, invest 25 quest points in the Pet Track at May's Quest Caravan and summon the Lorehound.

Red dye
  1. Tradeable = Yes
  2. Take three redberries and 5 coins to Aggie in Draynor Village.
  3. Ask her to mix you a red dye.

Alternatively, red dye can be bought from the Lletya Seamstress for 6 coins.

Raw bacon or cooked bacon (or any of the combined bacon items)
  1. Head to any pig pit and kill a pig, or buy bacon from the Grand Exchange.
Any item that causes Self-injury
Recommended: Zamorak brew

Tradeable = Yes

  • Any method that can bring life points below maximum such as equipment with a life bonus
  • Make the potion or buy it from the Grand Exchange, steal a rock cake from the rock cake stall in Gu'Tanoth, or let anything attack you. Then, with about 75% life points, use the Archaeology journal to teleport to the Archaeology Guild and use the Dig sites map to teleport to the Infernal Source.
    • Alternatively, you may equip an item that has a health bonus which will raise your maximum health to look as if you've been injured. Most Tank Armour has this effect.
Frog spawn
  1. Tradeable = No
  2. You'll need a (preferably closed) light source and a small fishing net for this step (a small fishing net added to the tool belt will work).
  3. Either visit the fishing spot at the far east of the Lumbridge Swamp Caves (requires a rope for first time visit) or the south-west fishing spot of the Dorgesh-Kaan South Dungeon
  4. With a net, fish out some tadpoles.
Desert disguise
  1. Tradeable = No
  2. Go to Ali Morrisane's shop at Al Kharid and buy a fake beard and a Kharidian headpiece.
  3. Combine these two items to form the wearable desert disguise.
Goblin potion
  1. Tradeable = No
  2. With a toadflax potion (unf) in your inventory, visit the Makeover Mage west of Falador and ask them for pharmakos berries (requires partial completion of Land of the Goblins quest.
  3. Combine the berries with the potion to create a goblin potion. Don't drink it immediately! You'll need it later.
Dragon bones or Baby dragon bones or Dragonkin bones

Tradeable = Yes

  • Kill any adult dragon or baby dragon. Alternatively, Dragonkin bones also work for this task.
  • Receive bones from a Modified first age tiara.
  • Buy the bones from the Grand Exchange.

The gargoyles[edit | edit source]

To complete this mystery, players must free each of the 13 gargoyles. This is done by speaking to them with a specific item in their inventory or by using a specific emote in order to acquire a rambling page from the gargoyle. Players must navigate through the portals within the Infernal Source in order to complete this mystery. The navigation portals are unreliable by default and often randomly teleport, therefore multiple attempts may be needed at the start to successfully navigate from one landmass to the other. Assisting Mephisto can reduce the chance of random teleportation down a minimum of 1/100, after freeing him seven times for the achievement Die! Die! Die!, or adding a restored chaos star to the toolbelt (requiring level 68 Archaeology) eliminates random teleportation.

  • Players may use the given map to navigate correctly to each of the gargoyle portals.
  • The gargoyles can be done in any order if you have the proper items.
  • Players will receive coins respective to the zodiac sign, so it is advised that they have inventory space available when doing the mystery, and that all gear is banked.
  • Players will need some Prayer points available for one of the gargoyles.

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • The player Sliske was the first player who completed this mystery on 30 March 2020 which was shown in-game with the announcement: News: Sliske is the first to solve the 'Contract Claws' mystery!
  • The Malebranche and their names (with the exception of Ophiuchus) are taken from the Divine Comedy, where they guard Bolgia Five in the eighth circle of Hell. Some of their dialogue and most of the requirements to speak with them refer to the meanings of their names in Italian.
    • Cagnazzo means "Nasty Dog". Draghignazzo refers to Cagnazzo as a nasty dog, and a dog is required to reach Cagnazzo.
    • Libicocco has been translated as "Libyan Hothead", and both Cagnazzo and Libicocco refer to it as a hothead, in addition to requiring the player to appear angry.
    • Alichino is derived from the Italian name for the character of Harlequin in commedia dell'arte; Libicocco refers to Alichino as "the harlequin", and Alichino wants a harlequin cow.
    • Rubicante means "Red-faced Terror", referenced both by the need to dye the gargoyle red and by Alichino and Graffiacane calling it "the red-faced one".
    • Graffiacane means "Scratcher of Dogs", which is how Rubicante refers to it. This may also be why Graffiacane is associated with the sign of Leo, a large, clawed feline.
    • Ciriatto means "Wild Hog". Ciriatto is called a pig by Graffiacane, oinks at the player, and must be given bacon to complete the mystery.
    • Scarmiglione has been translated as "Troublemaker", and Scarmiglione refers to itself as such, in addition to both Ciriatto and Scarmiglione alluding to the player "making/causing trouble" by blowing raspberries.
    • Malacoda means "Evil Tail", which is what Scarmiglione calls him, as well as being the reason he is associated with the sign of Scorpio.
    • Calcabrina has been translated as "Grace Stomper", and uses this title for itself.
    • Barbariccia means "Curly Beard", referenced by the gargoyle's fondness for beards.
    • Farfarello means "Goblin", which is what it and Barbariccia refer to it as, and what you must be in order to reach it.
    • Draghignazzo means "Nasty Sneering Dragon", and Draghignazzo is called a dragon by itself and Farfarello, as well as eating dragon bones.
  • When Alichino eats the player's harlequin cow, it says "I will not change my name. No, I am no troll." This is a reference to Baby Shakes, the other creature known to eat player-owned farm animals.
  • Malacoda's dialogue and love of frogspawn are a reference to the Russian fable of the Scorpion and the Frog.
  • Farfarello's musing over "red or green" is a reference to the Goblin Diplomacy quest.
