Delrith Again[edit | edit source]

Speak to Aris in New Varrock square. She tells you that you've got a future ahead of you, but you tell her that you already know that your future was to kill Delrith. She tells you that you need Silverlight to kill Delrith. You will recall from your first time killing Delrith that that Gideon Bede or Sir Prysin has the key to Silverlight. Aris will tell you that Gideon Bede is dead, but that Sir Prysin now lives in a house north-west of the church.

Finding the Key[edit | edit source]

Searching the drain.

Go to Sir Prysin's house, north-west of the church, just south of the northern gate. Ask him about the key you need. He says he had the key, but lost it. The key is in a random hiding spot inside, upstairs, or right outside the house in the drain to the south, and reappears in that location in further playthroughs. Go to the church and go down the trapdoor to the south-east of the entrance. Silverlight is covered by a barrier, without the 3 spirit guardians. Click on the barrier, and then go back to Aris, and tell her about this barrier. She will tell you that the guardian spirits have moved on, and you'll need to find them. She gives you a device. The ghosts are located in these locations, and you can visit them in any order you wish to. It is recommended that you go to the Spirit of the Mind ghost last, as he is closest to the church.

  • The first ghost is in the Blue Moon Inn, possessing Dr Harlow.
  • The second ghost is in the graveyard, to the north-west of Aris' tent. Inspect the tombstone stack along the north wall.
  • The third ghost is in a house, south of Sir Prysin's home, west of the church.

The three spirits[edit | edit source]

Spirit of the Body[edit | edit source]

Go to Dr Harlow and tell him he's possessed. You'll need a potion to sober him up, so the ghost can leave his body. Go to the Apothecary and ask if he can make a potion to sober someone up. He tells you that he can, but he needs a cabbage, a rotten tomato and a hair of a dog. He tells you that there is a dog in Gertrude's kennel.

The player is touching the kennel.
  • You can pick the cabbage from a garden south of the Blue Moon Inn.
  • You can get the rotten tomato from the crate north of the Blue Moon Inn.
  • Gertrude lives to the south of the western bank, close to the tanner, where the Zaros Emissary usually is. Next to her house, you see a kennel. Do every option available but do pulling your hand out last. You'll get a dog's hair when you finally remove your hand.

Return to the Apothecary. He'll take your ingredients and make the potion to sober someone up. Go back to Dr Harlow. If you try to give the potion to him, he'll refuse it. Buy a glass of beer from the bartender. Use the potion on the glass of beer, then give it to Dr Harlow. Keep the empty vial. The ghost will get out of his body, and Harlow gets angry at you for sobering him up. Talk to the ghost. He'll tell you that he wants you to kill anything in the bar. You can kill a zombie or alternatively, you may also ask him to kill yourself instead (you will not lose anything). As a third option, you can drink a Cadava potion acquired from the apothecary to trick the ghost. An additional fourth option exists, in which you may beat up Jonny the Beard as part of joining the Black Arm Gang for Shield of Arrav. Talk to the ghost again. He's happy, and tells you that his barrier is lifted. A short cutscene of his barrier disappearing will play.

Spirit of Faith[edit | edit source]

The tombstone stack next to the statue.

Go to the graveyard, north-west of Aris' tent, and go to the north side, next to the statue of Zemouregal. There is a "tombstone stack" that can be investigated. One of the three ghosts will pop out. Talk to him. He tells you to simply take a step to the right. If you ask him if it's your right or his right, he'll tell you he doesn't care. Simply take a step to the left or right, and talk to the ghost. He is satisfied (or bored) and lifts his barrier. A cutscene will appear.

Spirit of the Mind[edit | edit source]

An animated book attacks!

Go to the final ghost, in the house south of Sir Prysin's home. You'll see a bookcase moving at the south wall. Investigate the bookcase. An animated book will fight you. Kill it and the final ghost will appear. He asks you some trick questions, all options will work (since there are no true 'answers' to his questions). After that, he lifts his barrier. A cutscene will appear.

Dealing with Delrith[edit | edit source]

You can now go back to the crypt underneath the church and take Silverlight. You will be told that Delrith is in the stone circle southeast of the city. Before going there, buy some black robes from Thessalia's clothing shop for 90 zemomarks, and then fill the vial from the blood fountain east of the square. Continue to the red barrier north of the Zamorak temple and attempt to pass it, but Evil Dave won't let you for not being in his "evil" cult. To get in, you'll need to have an "awesome black weapon and robes", so pick a black mushroom from next to Evil Dave and use it to dye Silverlight. Ensure you are wearing Silverlight and the black robes and then speak to Evil Dave again to pass the barrier. The cultist outfit cosmetic override can substitute the robes.

Drawing a circle of blood for Delrith.

Talk to Delrith. He actually wants to be defeated and go back to Infernus. He'll ask you to simply stab him. You will automatically stab him, but a bolt of lightning will deflect the attack. Delrith tells you that that was Agrith Naar, and asks you to draw a blood circle. Go back to the Apothocary's home and search the desk. You will find a vial. Go back to the blood fountain east of Varrock Square, fill it with blood to get a Vial of blood. Talk to Delrith again and he will chant the spell to banish himself back to Infernus as your character draws the blood circle.

Defeating Agrith Naar[edit | edit source]

The battle with Agrith Naar

After this, Delrith disappears and Agrith Naar takes his place. The demon gets angry at you for sending him back to New Varrock and wants to fight you. Agrith scales to your level, so you need food and armour. If you're ready, you can accept. If not, you should gear up on some food and armour.

You can smith iron +1 armour at the forge at the west end of Varrock, and with a Crafting level of 20 and 50, rings of recoil and an Amulet of Strength can be made respectively. The required uncut sapphires and ruby can be bought from Urist's Crafting Supplies (New Varrock), the gold ore can be purchased from Sani and further processed into the jexelry using his furnace. A ball of wool can be bought from Thessalia's shop, and the required water, fire and cosmic runes to enchant the jewelry can be bought from Aubury. Gertrude in southwestern Varrock sells cakes for 150 Zemomarks as wells as stews for 170 Zemomarks that heal 170 HP and both stack and therefore don't take up much room in your backpack. Purchasing 10 of each should be enough. Silverlight is required to deal any damage and since an offhand weapon cannot deal damage a shield is recommended as well. Equip all of your gear and return to Agrith to begin combat.

Agrith attacks with magic and melee, as he did in Shadow of the Storm. He is also capable of telegrabbing you if you try and fight from a distance.

After Agrith's health has been lowered a bit he will begin to use a special attack similar to the one Delrith used in Demon Slayer. He'll scream "PREPARE TO BE INCINERATED!" and begin to charge an attack which does a massive amount of damage; run outside the circle of blood to avoid it. Eat some food if necessary, then resume fighting him.

After reducing his health again he will summon four lesser demons. These demons won't attack you; they will simply stand there and heal him. The demons are fairly weak, only having 800 health, but should be killed because Agrith is invulnerable to attacks while the demons remain alive.

Finally, as his health is lowered even further, he will use a final special attack and will scream "PREPARE TO BE INCINERATED" again. As before, just run a safe distance, then resume fighting him until he dies, and Silverlight will be upgraded into Darklight.

After killing Agrith, there is a chest hidden amongst the rubble and several wooden boxes which can be looted with a lockpick for the Pick All acheievement if you have completed the Shield of Arrav subquest.

Return to Aris and she'll tell you that Delrith and Agrith Naar weren't much of a threat anyway. She will still reward you for your troubles.

Congratulations! Quest complete!

Rewards[edit | edit source]

  • 3 quest points
  • 5,000 crafting XP lamp
  • 5,000 magic XP lamp
  • Access to ingredients for strength potions in the New Varrock demon camp
    • Only three strength potions may be made as the six ingredients, limpwurt root and red spiders' eggs, are obtained only once from looting the chest in the south-east corner of New Varrock
  • Always: Darklight
  • Always: 300 zemomarks
  • 2 Treasure Hunter keys (Ironman accounts will not receive these)

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • If you answer 0 in one of Spirit of the mind's questions he would have "lost connection"
  • After dealing with the demons, the player can talk to Evil Dave, and he asks the player what they did to his demon friends, to which the player replies "Dave, they're dead. They're all dead, Dave.". This is a reference to the show Red Dwarf, in which a computer program repetitively tells Dave Lister that everyone is dead.
  • The Graveyard spirit requests your character 'Take a Step to your Right', to which you can reply 'Nothing to do with my Hands and Hips?'. This is a reference to the film The Rocky Horror Picture Show
  • References to the old version of Demon Slayer:
    • Sir Prysin and Aris are both characters involved in the original Demon Slayer.
    • The drain is a possible location of the key, similar to how Sir Prysin dropped his key down the drain.
    • The adventurer asks "Which is it this time, Gideon Bede or Grüfeld Bach?" Grüfeld was the name of Gideon until 4 August 2014 when his character was significantly altered. Next, the adventurer says "Oh, so this is the one with Wally? So I'll need Sir Prysin's key - is he in the palace?" This is a reference to the original quest. Aris replies that the player definitely should not remember that one.
    • Delrith mentions that Denath summoned him and that he turned out to be a demon named Agrith Naar.
  • Gertrude's dog kennel is a reference to the 2012 Christmas event.
  • When you kill Delrith in the blood circle for the first time, he says, "Nullus... locus... est... similis... domui" which is Latin for, "There's no place like home", a reference to The Wizard of Oz.
  • After completing the quest, a replica of Silverlight will appear in the museum.
  • If the player is opting to join the Black Arm Gang and defeats Johnny the Beard while fetching Katrine's arm, the Spirit of the Body will be satisfied by Johnny's death, even though he isn't killed fully, strictly speaking.
